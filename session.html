      How this score is calculated

      The Y Combinator rubric · 6 lenses, 6 bands

      Anchored in PG essays, Sam Altman's playbook, and Michael Seibel's YC Startup School lectures.

      The 6 YC lenses

      • A · Make Something People Want — paying users beat survey data. (Market + Demand)
      • B · Default Alive vs Default Dead — does revenue cover burn? (Monetization)
      • C · Schlep Blindness — why hasn't a giant done this? (Market defensibility)
      • D · 10x Better, Not 10% — wedge has to be a step-change. (Interest)
      • E · Talk to Users — quote real users, not McKinsey. (Feasibility)
      • F · Tarpit Ideas — known graveyard patterns auto-fail. (Market)

      The 6 score bands

      • 90–100 YC-ready · would survive a Day-1 partner grilling
      • 75–89 Strong · fundable as-is at angel/pre-seed
      • 60–74 Watch · one lens fails — fix and re-pitch
      • 45–59 Weak · fails 2+ lenses — rewrite after 10 user calls
      • 30–44 Critical · fails 3+ lenses — won't attract tier-1 capital
      • 0–29 Tarpit · not a startup — kill it and pick something else

      Verdict mapping

      • ✓ Build it ≥ 4 dimensions in the 75+ band, no dimension below 50, monetization defensible
      • ~ Maybe mixed signals — strong on idea, weak on monetization or feasibility
      • ✕ Skip it 3+ dimensions below 55, OR any single dimension below 30, OR matches a tarpit pattern
